“Creativity thrives when commercial foundations are clear.”

Marketing procurement fails when it treats creativity like a commodity—and fails equally when it ignores commercial discipline. I see my role as a translator between creativity and control, ensuring budgets deliver outcomes without diluting brand impact.

Scope & Negotiation Levers

Category Scope

High-impact, brand-critical spend measured by outcomes.

  • Agencies: Creative, Branding, PR, Digital Performance.
  • Media: Buying & Planning (Digital, TV, OOH).
  • Events: Physical, Hybrid, Virtual, and Experiential.
  • Production: Promotional merchandise and print.

Key Negotiation Points

Commercial, Risk, and Scope Control levers.

  • Commercials: Transparent rate cards, performance-based fees, media rebates.
  • Scope Control: Clear SOWs to prevent scope creep, defined revision limits.
  • Rights & IP: Ownership of creative assets, global usage rights.
  • Risk (EU Lens): GDPR consent management, cancellation policies.

My Strategic Approach

1

Early Engagement

Partnering with Marketing early to align on objectives, not just spend. Protecting creativity by defining the sandbox.

2

Integrated Briefing

Standardized briefing packs, scope ladders, and approval gates keep agency resources focused on outcomes.

3

Commercial Architecture

Layering retainer vs project fees, rate cards, and KPI-linked incentives to align agency profitability with campaign ROI.

4

Performance + Governance

Quarterly business reviews, post-event audits, and content reuse dashboards to eliminate waste and surface learnings.
