Facilities Management
& IFM Strategy

Hard & Soft FM Energy & ESG Workplace Services

"Facilities is not a cost center—it’s a productivity, safety, and resilience engine."

I see FM as a business enabler, not an overhead. Poor facilities decisions show up as lost productivity, safety incidents, and reputational risk. My role is to ensure service reliability, compliance, and long-term cost control, not just vendor management.

Scope & Negotiation Levers

Category Scope

Services that keep the business running safely, efficiently, and sustainably.

  • Hard FM: HVAC, electrical, plumbing, fire safety, BMS.
  • Soft FM: Cleaning, security, waste, landscaping, catering.
  • Workplace: Front office, space management, mailroom.
  • Utilities: Energy management and consumption optimization.

Key Negotiation Points

Commercial, Contractual, and ESG levers.

  • Pricing Models: Output-based vs headcount-based billing.
  • Performance: KPI-linked SLAs (uptime, response time) with penalties.
  • Commercials: Indexation caps and gainshare for efficiency savings.
  • ESG (EU Lens): Workforce compliance, energy targets, carbon reporting.

My Strategic Approach

1

Demand Clarity First

Define service outcomes, not vague scopes. Ensure clear demarcation to avoid scope creep.

2

Performance-Led Contracts

SLAs must be tied to real business impact (e.g., asset uptime) rather than just activity (e.g., visits).

3

SBR Governance

Institutionalizing scorecards, quarterly reviews, and corrective action plans to drive vendor accountability.

4

Risk & Resilience

Planning for backups, business continuity, and disaster recovery for critical site operations.

Future of FM

Mastering FM with AI

Moving from reactive repairs to predictive optimization using data and AI.

  • Predictive Maintenance: AI flags failures before downtime.
  • Energy Analytics: Anomaly detection for consumption spikes.
  • Workforce Optimization: Match service levels to occupancy.
