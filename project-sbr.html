Context & Strategic Need

In a complex, multi-supplier environment, supplier management had become largely reactiveâ€”focused on issue resolution rather than structured performance improvement. Discussions were often subjective and disconnected from sourcing decisions, limiting Procurement's ability to drive long-term value.

The Shift

From Reactive Escalations ("Why is this late?") To Proactive Leadership ("How do we optimize joint value?").

Supplier Scorecard Design

At the core of the framework was a standardized, objective scorecard aligned with business objectives. Key dimensions included:

Quality

Defect rates, rejections, and rework. Compliance with technical specifications.

Delivery

On-time delivery (OTD), lead time adherence, and flexibility during demand spikes.

Cost & Commercials

Price competitiveness, TCO reduction initiatives (VAVE), and payment term adherence.

Service & Risk

Responsiveness, financial stability, ESG compliance, and capacity planning.

Strategic Impact

  • Performance Governance:

    Improved SLA adherence and service consistency across categories.

  • Vendor Consolidation:

    Supported ~30% vendor consolidation by identifying and exiting underperformers.

  • Resilience:

    Early identification of capacity risks and single-point-of-failure suppliers.

Strategic Value

"This initiative demonstrates my ability to institutionalize supplier performance management. By embedding scorecards into SBRs, I helped transform procurement into a performance governance function and a strategic decision partner."
