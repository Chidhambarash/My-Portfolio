“In HR procurement, cost savings mean nothing if people outcomes fail.”

I treat HR and professional services as capability enablers, not spend buckets. My responsibility is to protect the organization legally, ethically, and culturally, while ensuring services genuinely improve workforce effectiveness.

Scope & Negotiation Levers

Category Scope

High-impact, people-sensitive categories where experience outweighs unit cost.

  • Recruitment: RPO, agencies, contractors, MSP/VMS.
  • HR Tech: HRIS, LMS, payroll SaaS platforms.
  • Professional Services: Consulting, legal, audit, advisory.
  • EU Lens: GDPR, labor law compliance, ethical sourcing.

Key Negotiation Points

Commercial, Risk, and Flexibility levers.

  • Pricing: Outcome-based (e.g., time-to-hire), rate card governance.
  • Service Quality: Quality-of-hire guarantees, payroll accuracy SLAs.
  • Risk: Co-employment transfer, GDPR data processing agreements.
  • Flexibility: Scalability clauses for hiring freezes or surges.

My Strategic Approach

1

Deep HR Partnership

Shared goals, not transactional sourcing. Aligning procurement strategy with workforce planning and culture.

2

Risk-First Contracting

Prioritizing compliance, data privacy (GDPR), and correct worker classification over simple rate reduction.

3

Outcome-Driven Sourcing

Measuring impact (e.g., retention, successful project delivery) rather than just activity or effort.

4

Digital Enablement

Leveraging HR tech, analytics, and workflow automation to streamline onboarding and approvals.
