Freight & 3PL Fleet Lifecycle Emissions & ESG

“The true value of a logistics partner shows during disruption—not during normal operations.”

I treat logistics sourcing as risk engineering. Saving 2% on freight is irrelevant if a production line stops or customer commitments fail. My focus is predictability, resilience, and transparency, then cost optimisation.

Scope & Negotiation Levers

Category Scope

Ensuring business continuity by managing how goods, assets, and people move.

  • Freight: Road, Air, Sea, Rail transportation.
  • Services: Warehousing, 3PL, and Courier/Last-mile delivery.
  • Corporate Fleet: Leasing, maintenance, fuel, and telematics.
  • EU Lens: Emissions regulations (CBAM), labour compliance.

Key Negotiation Points

Cost Control, Service Reliability, and Resilience levers.

  • Pricing: Lane-based volume tiers, fuel surcharge indexation caps.
  • Service: OTD SLAs, capacity guarantees during peak demand.
  • Fleet TCO: Lease vs buy analysis, residual values, maintenance bundles.
  • Sustainability: EV transition clauses, emissions reporting.

My Strategic Approach

1

Visibility First

Understanding lanes, volumes, and seasonality before going to market. Mapping the network to identify optimization opportunities.

2

Core + Flex Model

Balancing leverage with resilience by maintaining strategic partners for baseload and flexible carriers for peak demand.

3

Precision Sourcing

Executing lane-level RFx rather than generic contracts. Governing fuel & inflation with strict indexation mechanisms.

4

Sustainability Roadmap

Driving EV adoption in fleets and reducing emissions through route optimization and modal shifts (rail/intermodal).
