"IT procurement is risk management disguised as purchasing."

I treat every IT deal as a balance of security, continuity, adoption, and cost. If the tool fails, is non-compliant, or locks the business in, any savings are wiped out. My job is to bring structure, leverage, and governance—without slowing innovation.

Scope & Negotiation Levers

Hardware & Lifecycle

Laptops, Desktops, Servers, Networking, AV, Spares, Asset Refresh.

  • Lifecycle Economics: Warranty length, accidental damage, swap/advance replacement.
  • Standardisation: Fewer SKUs = better pricing + simpler support.
  • End of Life (EoL): Buyback/trade-in programs and secure data disposal.
  • Sustainability: Repairability scores and energy efficiency ratings.

Software & SaaS

Enterprise Agreements, Security Tools, Collaboration Suites, Cloud Governance.

  • Licensing Flexibility: True-up/true-down, concurrent vs named user models.
  • Renewal Control: Caps on uplifts (CPI+/-), removal of auto-renew traps.
  • Compliance (EU Lens): GDPR data residency, subprocessor transparency.
  • Exit Strategy: Data portability, transition assistance, and escrow clauses.

My Strategic Approach

1

Demand Shaping

Aligning specs and standard configurations to reduce exceptions. Not just buying what is asked, but challenging the need.

2

Market Intelligence

Using rate cards, peer pricing, and cloud unit economics to build a "should-cost" view before negotiations.

3

TCO Business Case

Including implementation, training, support, and disposal costs—not just the license fee.

4

Consumption Control

Implementing usage analytics and approval guardrails to prevent "SaaS sprawl" post-contract.
